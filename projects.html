Expense

React: HTML, CSS, JavaScript

Expense is a budgeting web app, where users can manage their finances.
Some features include:

  • Setting monthly spending limits
  • Managing progress towards spending limit
  • Logging transactions
  • Visualizing expense trends

Key takeway: Thinking away from the keyboard and carefully mapping out relationships between components before diving into code not only results in more efficient code, but it also avoids a lot of debugging headaches.

Next steps: Implement server side with Express, Node.js, and MongoDB (MERN stack).

2D Maze Game

Java

This was the final project of CS61B, and it involves generating a random closed world with rooms and connecting hallways. The world included a torch feature, that displayed only a small radius of the world around the player and the objective of the game was to navigate through the maze to reach a target at the other side. This project involved graph algorithms and data structures (can't give away too much information here for future students).

Key takeway: Plan, plan, plan! As a partner project, it was even more crucial that we shared a clear understanding of our implementation. We spent hours drawing out different algorithms and writing pseudocode, which made dividing the tasks easier and avoided conflicting code.

Virtual Reality Research

Unity3D, C#

As part of the Student Science Training Program at the University of Florida, I conducted a study with over 100 participants and showcased the results in a research paper, a poster, and a presentation to other Engineering students, professors, post docs, and counselors. I explored whehter voice or animations is more responsible for the human resemblance of virtual humans.

Key takeway: True research takes many years of diving deep into the field in order to know enough to tackle the unknown. I spent weeks reading countless papers about the field before I finally formed my research topic taking inspiration from a paper describing the qualities responsible for the human resemblance of virtual humans.
